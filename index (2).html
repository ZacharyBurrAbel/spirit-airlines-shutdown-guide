Your situation — what you paid with matters most
Best position
Paid with a credit card
Spirit will auto-refund to your card. If that doesn't happen, call your issuer and request a "chargeback" under the Fair Credit Billing Act. Services were not delivered — you have a strong claim. Act within 120 days of your transaction.
Some protection
Paid with a debit card
Protections are weaker but worth trying. Call the number on the back of your debit card and ask about disputing the charge for non-delivery of service. Less likely to succeed than a credit card, but file before going to bankruptcy court.
Third-party booking
Booked via Expedia, Kayak, etc.
Spirit won't refund you directly. Contact your travel agency or booking platform and ask them to process your refund. Keep your Spirit confirmation number and proof of payment ready.
Uncertain — may not recover
Free Spirit points, vouchers, credits
Compensation "will be determined at a later date through bankruptcy court." Analysts say odds of recovering loyalty points are slim. You're an unsecured creditor — last in line. File a proof of claim to preserve any rights you have.
Not covered
Incidental costs (hotel, meals, car)
Spirit has explicitly stated it will not reimburse incidental travel costs. Check your travel insurance policy — some cover "insolvency" or "service cessation." Note: policies bought after Spirit's Aug 2025 bankruptcy filing may not qualify.

Your action checklist — in order of priority
1
Save all Spirit documentation immediately
Screenshot or download your confirmation email, booking receipt, any cancellation notice, and all Spirit correspondence. You'll need proof of your booking to access rescue fares and file claims.
2
Do not cancel your Spirit booking yourself
Let Spirit's cancellation stand. Voluntarily canceling a nonrefundable ticket eliminates your ability to file a chargeback — you must wait for Spirit's cancellation to be on record.
3
Check Spirit's refund status page (MyTrips)
Visit Spirit's website to see if an automatic refund has been initiated. Credit and debit card bookings made directly with Spirit should be refunded automatically to the original payment method.
4
If no refund appears, call your card issuer today
Tell them you want to dispute a charge for services not delivered (non-delivery of service). This is your right under the Fair Credit Billing Act. The sooner you call, the better — chargeback windows are strict.
5
Book a new flight if you still need to travel
Use the rescue fares listed above — they're the cheapest options right now. Also check Google Flights across all airlines. Be flexible on dates, connections, and nearby airports. Summer fares are rising fast.
6
If you paid with points, vouchers, or credits — file a bankruptcy claim
Contact Epiq, Spirit's claims agent, to file a formal "proof of claim." This preserves whatever recovery rights you may have in the bankruptcy proceedings. Recovery is not guaranteed and may take years.
7
Call your travel insurance provider
Ask specifically if your policy covers "airline insolvency" or "service cessation." If you purchased insurance after Spirit's August 2025 bankruptcy filing, your claim may be denied as a known event. Still worth calling.
8
File a DOT complaint if your rights are violated
If you believe your refund rights under federal law are not being honored, file a formal complaint with the U.S. Department of Transportation at dot.gov/airconsumer. The DOT is actively monitoring the situation.

What you are not entitled to
No extra compensation beyond your ticket price
Under current U.S. law, airlines are not required to compensate passengers for inconvenience, delays, or disruption caused by a shutdown. You can reclaim the cost of your ticket, but there is no entitlement to additional payments. Spirit has explicitly stated it cannot reimburse incidental travel costs.
